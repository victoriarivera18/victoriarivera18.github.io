My Small Auto-Biography

Born in Venezuela, I spent my early childhood in Anaco around my entire extended family. Around 2004-2005, my family moved away to the United States for the first time. Here, I learned English and spent the next 8 years of my life. We lived in Oklahoma and later California. 2013 was an important year for me because it was the year we moved away from the United States, the only home I could remember, to the Middle Eastern country, Kuwait. Culture shock was inevitable, but the Middle East was where I gained lifelong friends and was able to discover what the rest of the world had to offer. I loved learning about new cultures and it was here, ironically, where I grew to love and embrace my own Latin and Hispanic identity. These years were the best years of my life. But, not surprisingly, we moved yet again in 2016 to Houston, Texas. I remained here until coming to College Station to attend Texas A&M University. Initially a Mechanical Engineering major, it was in my introductory engineering course that I discovered a passion for programming and solving logical problems. After that, I didn't look back, and now I'm studying to attain my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science.

Interests and Hobbies

YouTube

{Excpetion Thrown} is a Youtube channel I created in April 2020 while I finished up my classes for the semester at home. The premise of my channel is to show what coding projects and assignments actually look like through the prespective of a college student. In my videos, I detail the struggle that all programmers face when writting and debugging code. I show myself Googling and reading documentation to give viewers and realistic look at the day-to-day of solving a coding problem.

Options Trading

One of my life goals is to become finacially independent at a young age. I became obsessed with this idea aroung the age of 18 and started looked for ways to grow finacially. Options trading is one of the means to this end. Using Robinhood and a relatively small amouny of capital, I trade using strategies devised by Colombian economist Alejandro Cardona. Right now, I look to make a 5% gain of my total account per week. Eventually, I do hope to gain confidence and trade more frequently and effectively.
